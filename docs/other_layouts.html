I've made some other layouts besides Semimak. Some of them are pretty neat, but they haven't been very public besides floating around Discord. Mostly, the great ec0vid wanted to have an official reference for the Keyboard Layouts Doc, so here's that.

Rolll

y o u w b  x k c l v
i a e n p  d h s r t
j / , . q  f m g ' z

I made Rolll after Semimak. It's called Rolll because it's basically Semimak but it has more rolls, particularly inward rolls. It also has less redirects. There's not much more to it other than that. If you mirror the layout, it's pretty similar to Semimak. The main possible issue for people would be the vtz right pinky. And of course, some of the same problems as Semimak, like the lr' ring and the e, SFB.

In terms of how good it is overall, it's better than Semimak in terms of the relationships between different fingers; the roll/redirect ratio is quite good. It utilizes the index fingers more, which most people would probably like. However, it does have worse same-finger statistics than Semimak.

Hyperroll

p c l m v  x u o y f
n s r t d  . a e i h
b g ' w z  / , q j k

Like Rolll, this one has a lot of rolls, except it has very few outward rolls and redirects. Hyperroll actually looks pretty nice overall, but the pinky movement might be a bit much for most people.

Flaw

f l a w p  z k u r /
h s o y c  m t e n i
b j ' g v  q d . x ,

Flaw is just a layout I stumbled across while making Semimak. It's funny because it says "flaw" on the top. That's pretty much where the interesting stuff about it ends.

Less serious layouts

Foalmak

b x , w v  z / u t k
f o a l s  n e i g h
p ' . m c  q j y d r

I made Foalmak for the Colemak Discord's "April's Foal" day in 2021. It says "foals neigh" on the homerow. Pretty funny. It's even in EPKL.

Vitrimak

Staggered 

t k v u m  i a j b r
 w x / f p  d g q , s
  h . ' c o  l n z y e

Ortho 

t y q o c  a i v . e
w , x p m  g d / j s
h b z u f  n l ' k r

Vitrimak is an attempt at making the "worst" keyboard layout, similar to CarpalX's TNWMLC layout. It only used finger speed as a metric. It's not bad at being bad.
